Who am I?

Hello! My name is Taieb Oussaifi. I was born in September 1995 in Menzah VI, Ariana, Tunisia. I grew up in Rades Mont-Jilles, a coastal charming city on the southern suburbs of Tunis. I currently live and work in Saarbrucken, Germany.

I hold a bachelor's degree in English Studies (major: linguistics; minor: literary studies) from the University of Tunis and a master's degree in North American Studies (major: political science; minor: economics) from the University of Bonn. I am trained in North American domestic and foreign policy, micro- and macroeconomics and global political economy.

I have a well established professional experience in managing and coordinating research projects, in particular EU-funded research grants.

I am currently taking over the responsibilities of management, coordination and communication of the ERC Synergy Grant "DynAMiCs" at the Max Planck Institute for Software Systems under the supervision of Prof. Joel Ouaknine.

10/2025-Present: DynAMiCs - Dynamical and Arithmetical Model Checking

Project Description:

The EU-funded DynAMiCs project will combine tools from different areas of computational mathematics such as number theory, Algorithmic Verification and Symbolic Dynamics to study discrete dynamical systems. The project is funded by the European Union under the program European Research Council (ERC) through the funding scheme Horizon ERC Synergy Grants (Grant ID: 101167561)"

02/2022-08/2023: Teach About U.S.// Going Green - Education for Sustainability

Project Description:

Teach About U.S. was a cooperation between the U.S. Embassy Berlin, Leuphana University Lüneburg and LIFE e.V. Teach About U.S. was an award-winning intercultural blended learning platform that enriched teaching in American Studies, English and Education for Sustainable Development. Special projects such as “Going Green”, “Power to the People,” "U.S. Elections" and “Humboldt 250” brought innovative teaching approaches into German classrooms. Highlights were the U.S. Embassy School Election Projects 2012, 2016, 2020 and 2024. The project has had more than 20,000 participants in Germany and the USA over the past 13 years.
